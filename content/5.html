What is metabolic syndrome?


Metabolic syndrome is a serious condition that can have a major impact on your health, and is
characterized by a combination of high blood pressure, high blood sugar, excess body fat around
the waist, and abnormal cholesterol levels. The risk of developing heart disease, stroke, and
diabetes increases significantly when you have metabolic syndrome. In order to reduce your risk
of developing metabolic syndrome, itâ€™s important to maintain a healthy lifestyle, including
regular physical activity and a balanced diet. As a dietitian, I focus on helping people with
metabolic syndrome make lifestyle changes that are sustainable and that will help reduce their
risk factors. This includes making changes to your diet such as reducing your intake of processed
and sugary foods, increasing your consumption of fresh fruits and vegetables, and eating lean
proteins. Additionally, I provide strategies for increasing physical activity, such as walking
or joining a gym. By making these lifestyle changes, you can lower your risk of developing
metabolic syndrome, and reduce the risks associated with it.
